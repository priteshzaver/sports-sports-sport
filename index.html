Football is fun!

There are three phases to every football game

1. Offense

2. Defense

3. Special Teams

Offense:

The goal of the offense is to score as many points as possible. The offense can score one of two ways:

Touchdown!

A touchdown is worth 7 points and the offense scores a touchdown when the ball carrier carries the ball across the goal line.

Field Goal!

If the offense gets close enough, but fails to score a touchdown, the offense may elect to kick a field goal. The center snaps the ball to a holder, who holds the ball in an upright fashion so that the kicker can strike the ball. If the ball carries between two yellow upright poles, the offense is awarded 3 points.

Defense:

The goal of the defense is to keep the Offense from scoring a touchdown or field goal

The Defense gets 4 chances to keep the offense from advancing the ball 10 yards.

If the Offense is not in a position to score, on the fourth attempt,(down), the offense can use the fourth attempt to gain the 10 yards, or punt it to the opposing team.

Special Teams

Special teams consists of:

kicking field goals:

If a kicker succesfully kicks the ball through the opposing teams uprights, that team is awarded 3 points

punting:

If the offense fails to gain 10 yards in 3 tries, that team can kick the ball back to the opposing team so the opposing team has fiurther to go for a field goal or touchdown.

kickoff and punt coverage:

Special teams is responsible for tackiling the ball carrier that catches the kickoffs and punts.

kickoff and punt return:

A team may catch a punt or kickoff and try to advance the ball as far as possible on the playing field.
